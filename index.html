About INONE 2FA

INONE 2FA is a modern, offline-first, secure, and open-source Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) application. It is designed to act as a reliable shield for your online accounts, generating time-based one-time passwords (TOTP) and counter-based one-time passwords (HOTP) to defend against unauthorized access.

Your privacy is absolute. This application operates entirely on-device, prioritizing security without compromise.

1. Information Collection and Storage

Our app does not require a user account or any form of registration. No personal data is collected or transmitted. Everything you generate is stored locally in an encrypted database on your device.

Zero Cloud Connection: The application does not upload your secret keys or backups to any external servers. Your vault security depends entirely on the physical safety of your device and the strength of the vault password you select during initial setup.

2. Encryption Standards

The local database containing your secret keys is encrypted using the Advanced Encryption Standard (AES). This ensures that even if someone extracts the raw database files from your device, they cannot decrypt the content without knowing your master password.

3. Device Permissions and How They Are Used

INONE 2FA requests access to specific device capabilities to perform its core functions. Here is exactly why these permissions are needed:

  • Camera: Required to scan QR codes during account setup. This allows the application to read the authentication secret key encoded in the barcode instantly. Visual streams are processed in-memory and never saved or shared.
  • Biometric Credentials: Required if you decide to unlock the application with your fingerprint or face instead of typing your password. Authentication is completed securely via the Android BiometricPrompt API; the app does not store or see your biometric descriptors.

4. User Guide: How to Use INONE 2FA

Setting up and using INONE 2FA is straightforward. Follow these steps to secure your online presence:

Step 1: Welcome & Setup
When you first open INONE 2FA, you will see the welcome screens. Set up a secure lock screen password or enable biometrics to lock your app's vault.

Step 2: Adding an Account
To add a service (e.g., Google, GitHub, Discord) to INONE 2FA:
- Open the security settings of the respective service and select "Enable 2FA".
- Scan the QR code shown on their screen using the INONE 2FA camera scanner.
- Alternatively, copy their manual secret key and paste it directly into the manual entry form in the app.

Step 3: Verification
Once added, a 6-digit verification code will start generating on your screen. The code updates automatically every 30 seconds. Enter this code on the website to finish binding your account.

Step 4: Backing Up Your Accounts
Since the app is fully offline, if you lose your phone, you lose access to your codes. We highly recommend creating regular encrypted backups:
- Go to App Settings > Export.
- Create an encrypted backup file, choose a secure password, and store this backup file safely on a secure physical drive or private storage.

5. Third-Party Libraries and Open Source

This application is open-source. It does not integrate with any analytics libraries, proprietary advertising kits, or trackers. There are no cookies, beacons, or background reporting threads running inside the app.
