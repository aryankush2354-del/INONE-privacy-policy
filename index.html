Privacy Policy • Last updated: June 06, 2026

INONE 2FA is built as a free, secure, and open-source Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) application. This service is provided at no cost and is designed to be used as is.

Your privacy is of utmost importance to us. This policy describes how we handle information in the INONE 2FA mobile application.

1. Information Collection and Use

INONE 2FA is fully offline-first. We do not collect, store, upload, or transmit any of your personal data, credentials, account tokens, secret keys, or authentication records. All database vaults are encrypted using strong encryption standards and remain exclusively on your local device storage.

2. Device Permissions Required

To provide core security features, the app requests the following device permissions:

  • Camera Permission: Used solely to scan QR codes for adding new two-factor authentication accounts to the application. Camera inputs are processed in-memory locally on the device and never sent to any remote servers.
  • Biometric (Fingerprint/Face) Authentication: Used only to secure the application entry and unlock the vault locally. Biometric data is managed by the Android operating system secure enclave, and the app never accesses or stores your biometric template.

3. Third-Party Services

The application does not use any third-party software development kits (SDKs), advertising networks, analytics platforms, or background tracking systems. No analytics data, crash reports, or device identifiers are shared with third parties.

4. Security

We value your trust in using our app for securing your credentials, thus we use commercially acceptable security practices to protect your data locally. However, please remember that no method of transmission over the internet, or method of electronic storage is 100% secure and reliable, and we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

5. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. Thus, you are advised to review this page periodically for any changes. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page.
